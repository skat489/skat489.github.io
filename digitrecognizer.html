I'm a student at Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts.

My journey into machine learning started when I felt completely lost. I began with basic programming and, over time, taught myself Python from scratch. Fueled by countless YouTube tutorials and the invaluable lessons from Harvard's CS50 course, I slowly began to see the bigger picture.

I dove deep into the world of neural networks—experimenting with tools like the MLPClassifier and Standard Scalers—to build my very own digit recognizer. There were moments of doubt and confusion, but every challenge pushed me to learn more and explore further.

Through perseverance, I combined my newfound knowledge with practical experiments, creating a project that not only recognizes hand-drawn digits but also symbolizes my growth as a developer. This digit recognizer represents the culmination of hard work, passion, and a relentless pursuit of knowledge.
