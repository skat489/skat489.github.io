All About My Digit Recognizer

I'm a student at Phillips Academy in Andover, Massachusetts.

When I first saw this project I was completely lost but something inside of me was determined to get it done and to see how proud I would be to make something like this would be so worth it.

I knew I had to use neural networks but I didn’t even know what they were and how they worked. So before even writing a line of code I spent days learning about neural networks, MLP classifiers, standard scalers all by myself. I taught myself these tools through youtube and courses such as CS50 Harvard course. Through many trials I finally got it. I trained machine learning to guess the blurred number shown. At first it wasn’t accurate which drove me crazy but after focusing and looking back all i had to do was tweak the training data and the iterations. I was done.

Not yet. After a week of daily effort you would think I would be done. But a digit recognizer is an average code and I wanted to do something different and cooler. With my own creativity I wanted the user to draw a number instead of the machine giving it out. At this point it was only a thought, no idea, no knowledge, just a dream. After using AI to help me to understand what I have to do, I was even more motivated. I knew I was so close but so far. Remembering from my CS class I took at Phillips Academy last term I remembered about TkInter. With this in mind the next day I went to my old CS teacher and asked her to teach me about tkinter once more. Now I was getting somewhere, I had the idea and logic down. I went back to VS Code and youtube and learned about PIL. Finally I can call it a success. I was proud of my work and was more motivated than ever.
